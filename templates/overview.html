Conclusions

  • Upon visual inspection, areas with a higher density of fast-food restaurants have a lower unemployment rate.
  • The relationship between poverty and unemployment rate was statistically insignificant.
  • More rural counties have higher unemployment rates.
  • One major limitation: The number of restaurants in smaller cities using Yelp.
  • Possible Type I: Hypothesis Error
  • These results are opposite of initial thoughts.

    • Further Research:
    • Look at the movement of people (roadways, large cities, etc.) or population density in comparison to fast-food restaurants.
    • Find a more comprehensive list of restaurants.
    • Break down locations by zip code.
